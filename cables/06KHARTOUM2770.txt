SUBJECT:  Sudan:  MFA Advises of Lifting of Travel Restrictions for 
U.S. Citizens and USG Officials 
 
Ref:  A) Khartoum 02408, B) Khartoum 02410 
 
¶1.  On November 30, the Embassy received a note verbale from the 
Ministry of Foreign Affairs lifting the 25-mile travel limit placed 
September 27 (reftels) on all U.S. citizens in Sudan, other than 
those assigned to the mission. 
 
¶2.  The text of the note verbale, received in English, is as 
follows: 
 
(Begin text) 
 
The Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the Republic of the Sudan 
presents its compliments to the Embassy of the United States of 
America in Khartoum and with reference to the Ministry's Note verbal 
No. MFA/AMER/7/1/1/277 dated 26th September 2006 regarding the 
restriction imposed on the movement of all U.S. citizens traveling 
to Sudan within the limit of 25 mile radius from the Republican 
Palace in Khartoum, has the honor to convey to the esteemed Embassy 
that the above-mentioned restriction has been lifted with immediate 
effect. 
 
(Complimentary close) 
 
(End text) 
 
¶3.  Post is issuing a revised warden notice to this effect and 
proposing revised language to the Department for a revised Consular 
Information Sheet (CIS) on Sudan.
