SUBJECT: LAUNCH OF SPACE SHUTTLE MISSION STS-119 
 
¶1.  SPACE SHUTTLE MISSION STS-119, DISCOVERY (OV-103), 
WILL LAUNCH FROM KENNEDY SPACE CENTER (KSC), FLORIDA, NO 
EARLIER THAN MARCH 11, 2009.  THE LAUNCH WINDOW OPENS AT 
09:15 PM EDT, MARCH 11 (MARCH 12, 01:15 UTC/GMT) AND IS 
10 MINUTES LONG.  NOMINAL END OF MISSION LANDING AT KSC 
IS SCHEDULED FOR NO EARLIER THAN MARCH 25, 2009 AT 03:27 
PM EDT (19:27 UTC/GMT). 
 
¶2.  ZARAGOZA AIR BASE, SPAIN IS THE PRIMARY TRANSOCEANIC 
ABORT LANDING SITE (TAL). MORON AIR BASE, SPAIN, AND 
ISTRES AIR BASE, FRANCE, ARE THE TWO ALTERNATE SITES. 
 
¶3.  OES/SAT WILL STAFF THE JOINT STATE-NASA SHUTTLE 
MONITORING GROUP IN THE DEPARTMENT'S OPERATIONS CENTER 
FOR THE LIFT-OFF PERIOD.  THE OPERATIONS CENTER WILL 
ESTABLISH A CONFERENCE CALL TELEPHONE LINK BETWEEN THE 
MONITORING GROUP AND SHUTTLE CONTROL OFFICERS AT EMBASSY 
MADRID AND EMBASSY PARIS, AT APPROX. ONE HALF HOUR PRIOR 
TO LIFT-OFF.  SHUTTLE MONITORING GROUP CONTROL OFFICERS 
ARE OES/SAT, FERNANDO ECHAVARRIA AND DORIETHA JACKSON, 
AND GARVEY MCINTOSH, NASA.
