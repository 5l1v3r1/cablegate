SUBJECT: MGSU01:  MONITORING GROUP FORMATION REGARDING 
THE SITUATION IN SUDAN 
 
¶1.  As of 1700 EST on 03/03/09 the Executive Secretary 
directed the Bureau of African Affairs to establish a 
Monitoring Group regarding the situation in Sudan.  The 
Monitoring Group will operate virtually with support 
from the Operations Center, but will be prepared to 
upgrade to physical staffing should events warrant. 
 
¶2.  The Monitoring Group Coordinator will be Pamela 
Fierst of the Bureau of African Affairs (AF/SPG). 
Representatives from AF, CA, DS, S/WCI, PMAT, IO, P, M, 
and USAID will participate in the Monitoring Group; 
additional bureaus or offices may be added to the group 
as needed. 
 
¶3.  All cables and other documents relating to this 
Monitoring Group should include the SIPDIS caption. 
Reporting cables should include MGSU01 in the subject 
line as well as the following tags: AEMR, ASEC, CASC, 
KFLO, MARR, PREL, PINR, AMGT, SU. 
 
¶4.  A Sudan classified webpage, which includes 
Monitoring Group situation reports, is available at 
(http://www.intelink.sgov.gov/wiki/Portal:Cris is_Sudan). 
Please note that this web address is case sensitive. 
 
¶5.  Embassy Khartoum should take the following actions 
immediately if they have not already been completed: 
 
(a)  Provide by email the office and residence phone 
numbers of all country team members and other key 
officers who will have action responsibilities.  Please 
direct these emails to SES-O@state.gov. 
 
(b)  Participate in daily conference calls and provide 
situation reports by email or cable, as required, for as 
long as the Monitoring Group is in operation. 
 
(c)  Review post's Emergency Action Plan (including 
tripwires), update the F-77 report of potential 
evacuees, and ensure the Evacuation Management System 
(EMS) contains the most recent information on personnel 
present at post. 
 
¶6.  Minimize considered.
