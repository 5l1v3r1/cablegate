SUBJECT:  IRAQI MEDIA PLACEMENT OF PRESIDENT'S RAMADAN GREETING 
 
¶1. (U) Summary: President Obama's special Ramadan greeting to the 
Muslim world was carried in several Iraqi newspapers and news 
websites, as well as on three Iraqi Television channels that 
broadcast throughout the region.  Reporting of the greeting was 
immediate and positive.  A number of news outlets underscored the 
President's message on ending the Iraq war responsibly, and starting 
a new chapter in U.S.-Muslim relations around the world. End 
Summary. 
 
¶2. (U) The following is a list of placements generated as a result 
of post's outreach efforts: 
 
 
PRINT PRESS 
 
Ad-Dastoor (second most widely read Arabic daily), August 23. Front 
page news bar headlined: "Obama and Muslims," focuses on the 
President's pledge to begin anew with the Muslim world. 
 
Az-Zaman (independent, privately-owned daily), August 23. Page 2 
headline reads: "On the occasion of Ramadan, Obama Promises to End 
War." 
 
 
Al-Ittihad (pro-Kurdish Arabic language daily), August 23. Page 3 
headline reads: "Obama congratulates world's Muslims in Arabic." 
Full length article on President Obama's "commitment to build a 
safer and more secure world." 
 
TV AND WEB COVERAGE 
 
Al-Sumariya TV (Evening newscast of August 22). 
Crawler: "President Obama promises tangible measures to renew U.S. 
relations with Islamic world." 
 
Al-Sharqiya TV Website: 
Caption: "Obama Addresses Muslims on Occasion of Ramadan." 
 
Al-Hurra Iraq TV: Recurring crawler: "U.S. commends role of Islam in 
advancing justice, progress, tolerance and human dignity." 
 
Asharq Al-Awsat Newspaper Website: 
Caption: "Obama reemphasizes commitment to "new relations" with 
Muslims to achieve mutual goals." 
 
 
Al Malaf Press News Agency Website: "Obama addresses Muslims for 
Ramadan, vows to end the war in Iraq, and urges two-state solution 
for Israel-Palestine... re-emphasizes commitment to new relations 
with Muslims to achieve mutual goals."
