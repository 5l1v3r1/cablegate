¶1. This is an Action Request.  See paragraphs 2-4. 
 
¶2. The Department's Office of Defense Trade Controls 
Compliance (PM/DTCC) requests Posts' assistance in conducting 
a post-shipment end-use check on license number 05-050031210 
for the export of defense hardware to Singapore.  Posts are 
requested to complete this Blue Lantern inquiry within 45 
days.  Lack of response to a Blue Lantern inquiry will affect 
pending and future licenses involving parties to this license. 
 
¶3. Reason for request:  Unfamiliar foreign intermediate 
consignee and foreign consignee. 
 
¶4. Action Pretoria and Singapore:  Department requests that 
Posts make inquiries to establish the bona fides of South 
African-based foreign consignee Poynting Antennas and 
Singapore-based foreign intermediate consignee Stelop, 
respectively.  Conduct site visits to assess the companies' 
reliability as recipients of United States Munitions List 
(USML) articles as well as their operations and on-site 
security.  If site visits are not possible, please inform 
PM/DTCC case officer.  Remind the companies that the 
unauthorized re-export or retransfer of USML items is 
strictly prohibited. 
 
The following questions provide suggested guidance: 
 
 (To Stelop and Poynting Antennas) 
 
 - Please confirm that an order was shipped under license 
05-050031210. 
 
 - How long has your company been in the business of 
importing and selling defense articles? In what other name(s) 
has your company done 
business, if any? 
 
 - Are you familiar with the restrictions against the 
unauthorized re-export or retransfer of U.S.-origin defense 
articles? 
 
 - What other products are sold by your company? 
 
 - Who are your regular customers and where are they located? 
 
 
(Singapore: Ministry of Home Affairs) 
 
 - Please confirm that you received the items listed on 
license 
05-050031210 and that they are being used for the stated 
purpose. 
 
 - How long has the Ministry of Home Affairs done business 
with Stelop? 
 
 - What were the specific roles of Stelop and Poynting in the 
transaction described in No. 5, below? 
 
End Action Request.
