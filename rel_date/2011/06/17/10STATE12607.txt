VIP TRAVEL 
------------- 
 
¶1. (SBU)  DoD has established a weekly, Friday Codel flight 
which can accommodate up to eight Senators, eight 
Representatives, two House staff and two Senate staff.  The 
visit will allow for about 4-5 hours on the ground in Haiti 
with visits to a relief site and a U.S. troop site.  The first 
scheduled weekly trip has 10 commitments and is departing 
early February 12.  (TF1/Embassy Port-au-Prince Sitrep) 
 
EVACUATIONS/AMERICAN CITIZEN ISSUES 
-------------------------------------- 
 
¶2. (SBU) Since February 10 at 2000 EST, 88 American evacuees 
and family members have departed Port-au-Prince on seven 
flights bound for Charleston and Miami; a total of 16,218 have 
been evacuated to date.  There are currently 45 evacuees at 
the airport.  (TF1/Embassy Port-au-Prince Sitrep) 
 
¶3. (SBU) Embassy Port-au-Prince processed about 90 immigrant 
visas on February 10.  The Embassy reports there are about 350 
remaining cases from January and early February, and expects 
to interview about 80 and 100 cases each day for the remainder 
of the month. (CA shift report 85) 
 
¶4. (SBU)  As of February 10 at 2000 EST, there have been  no 
additional confirmed American citizen fatalities. 
(TF1/Embassy Port-au-Prince Sitrep) 
 
HUMANITARIAN UPDATE 
----------------------- 
 
¶5. (SBU) On February 10, distributions took place at 15 of 16 
fixed sites.  Since the earthquake, the U.N. World Food 
Program (WFP) and partners have provided emergency food 
assistance to more than 2.5 million people, including more 
than 1.5 million people to date through the 16-site 
distribution system.  (TF1/USAID Sitrep #53) 
 
MEDICAL RESPONSE 
 
¶6. (SBU) USNS COMFORT reports that 131 patients, of whom 91 
were American, have been evacuated.  The Comfort has seen 
6,932 outpatients.  They have 1,169 inpatients, of whom 974 
are surgical cases.  (IA ConfCall, 1800, 2/9) 
 
OTHER MAJOR DEVELOPMENTS 
 
¶7. (SBU) THE GOVERNMENT OF HAITI (GOH) DECLARED THREE DAYS OF 
MOURNING TO BEGIN FEBRUARY12. (TF1/EMBASSY PORT AU PRINCE 
INTERAGENCY CALL). 

¶8. (SBU) On February 10, Unified Coordinator for Disaster  
Response Ambassador Lewis Lucke met with President Pr,val to 
discuss the GoH plan for reconstruction and efforts to restore 
GoH capacity, specifically the potential relocation of GoH 
offices and staff to the former U.S. Embassy and USAID 
buildings.  They also planned to discuss GoH strategies for 
rubble removal and displaced population resettlement. (USAID, 
0530, 2/10) 
 
¶9.  (SBU) Commercial flights to PAP airport will resume soon. 
The Government of Haiti (GOH) requested additional food 
distribution sites in Croix des Bouquets and Kenscoff ? a 
needs assessment will begin today.  The USNS COMFORT will 
conduct a site survey on locations that may be able to provide 
neo-natal care ? if located sites meet the requirements, 
COMFORT will close their neo-natal ward. (DHS sitrep) 
 
¶10. (SBU) Consular Affairs announced that they are shutting 
down the the CA Port-Au-Prince control room effective 2:00 pm 
Thursday, February 11.  (TF1 Email)
