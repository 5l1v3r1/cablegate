SUBJECT: BIO DATA REPORT: FRANCIS, MAYANN, NOVA SCOTIA'S NEW 
LIEUTENANT GOVERNOR 
 
REF: HALIFAX 0115 
 
¶1. (U) Per reftel Prime Minister Harper has named Mayann Francis 
to be Nova Scotia's new Lieutenant Governor.  Following is 
biographic material on Ms. Francis who will be sworn in on 
September 7, 2006. 
 
¶2. (U) NAME: Mayann Elizabeth FRANCIS 
 
¶3. (SBU) DATE OF BIRTH: February 18, 1946 
 
¶4. (U) PLACE OF BIRTH: Sydney (Whitney Pier), Nova Scotia, 
Canada 
 
¶5. (SBU) MARITAL STATUS:  Unmarried 
 
¶6. (U) FAMILY: daughter of Archpriest George A. Francis and 
Thelma D. Francis 
 
¶7. (U) FUTURE POSITION: To assume the position of Lieutenant 
Governor of the Province of Nova Scotia, Canada, September 7, 
2006 
 
¶8. (U) PAST POSITIONS: 
-- 1999- 2006 - Chief Executive Officer, the Nova Scotia Human 
Rights Commission 
-- 2000 - 2003 - Nova Scotia Ombudsman (concurrent with the 
position of CEO of the Human Rights Commission) 
-- Senior-level positions in the Ontario government, Ministry of 
Municipal Affairs and Housing and the Ontario Women's 
Directorate as an Assistant Deputy Minister. 
-- Administrative Manager with the Office of the District 
Attorney in Kings County, New York 
-- Human Rights Officer, N.S. Human Rights Commission 
-- Employment Equity Officer, Dalhousie University, Halifax 
 
¶9. (U) EDUCATION: 
-- 2003: Certificate in Theological Studies, Atlantic School of 
Theology, Halifax, Nova Scotia 
-- 1984: Master's degree in Public Administration, New York 
University 
-- 1972: Bachelor of Arts Degree, St. Mary's University, 
Halifax, Nova Scotia 
 
¶10. (U) AWARDS: 
-- 2000: Harry Jerome Award (awarded by the Black Business and 
Professional Association) recognizing excellence in management 
and leadership in a profession. 
-- Honored by the Multicultural Education Council of Nova Scotia 
-- Awarded Golden Jubilee Medal 
-- Recipient of the Silver Plaque Award from the N.S. Human 
Rights Commissions, recognizing outstanding contribution in the 
field of human rights. 
 
¶11. (U) MEMBERSHIPS: Member of numerous community, educa-tion, 
business, and service organizations 
 
¶12. (U) DISTINCTIONS: First Black Lieutenant Governor for Nova 
Scotia; first female Ombudsman for Nova Scotia 
 
¶13. (U) ACKNOWLEDGEMENTS: Profiled in numerous publica-tions, 
including Millennium Minds, One hundred Black Canadians, the 
Who's Who in Black Canada, Canadian Who's Who and the Globe and 
Mail. 
 
¶14. (U) PUBLICATIONS: Monthly columnist for the Halifax 
"Chronicle Herald"
