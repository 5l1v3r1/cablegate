¶1.  (S/NF)  WASHINGTON ANALYSTS GREATLY APPRECIATE POST'S 
CABLES THAT DETAIL THE COMPLEXITIES AND PROGRESS OF THE 
BRAZILIAN AIR FORCE'S (FAB) F-X2 FIGHTER COMPETITION 
INVOLVING US, FRENCH, AND SWEDISH AIRCRAFT.  ANALYSTS HAVE 
USED THIS REPORTING IN BRIEFINGS AND OTHER PRODUCTS, TO 
INCLUDE A GRAPHIC HIGHLIGHTING A TIMELINE THAT CHARTED THE 
FAB'S QUEST FOR A NEW FIGHTER OVER THE PAST EIGHT YEARS. 
ANALYSTS HAVE BENEFITED FROM THE TIMELINESS OF POST'S 
REPORTING AND ITS ABILITY TO KEEP ABREAST OF DEVELOPMENTS 
BETWEEN THE MINISTRY OF DEFENSE, AIR FORCE, AND F-X2 PROGRAM 
OFFICIALS.  POST'S INSIGHTS INTO THE RECENT BRAZILIAN-FRENCH 
DEFENSE AGREEMENT AND ITS IMPACT ON THE COMPETITION, 
PARTICULARLY THE ROLE AND INTERACTION OF FRANCE'S DASSAULT 
AEROSPACE COMPANY WITH BRAZILIAN OFFICIALS AND BUSINESSES, 
WERE ALSO ARE EXTREMELY USEFUL. 
 
¶2.  (S/NF)  WASHINGTON ANALYSTS APPRECIATE POST'S REPORTING 
THAT HAS IDENTIFIED CHANGES WITHIN THE COMPETITION AND 
DEFENSE MINISTRY OFFICIALS' POLICIES REGARDING TECHNOLOGY 
SHARING AGREEMENTS,  THE IMPACT OF BRAZIL'S ECONOMIC DOWNTURN 
ON THE F-X2 PROGRAM, ATTITUDES OF FAB OFFICIALS TOWARDS THE 
PRICE AND AVAILABILITY OF THE FINAL THREE CONTENDING 
AIRCRAFT, AND THE CURRENT STATUS OF THE US FIGHTER AND ITS 
MAIN COMPETITOR, THE DASSAULT RAFALE.  MANY THANKS AGAIN FOR 
AN OUTSTANDING JOB OF TRACKING AN OFTEN COMPLEX COMPETITION 
AND BRAZILIAN DEFENSE ISSUES.  KIND REGARDS FROM WASHINGTON.
