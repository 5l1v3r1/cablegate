VIP TRAVEL 
---------- 
 
¶1.      (U) UNSYG Ban is en route to Port-au-Prince, and will 
meet with President Preval. (AP) 
 
¶2.      (U) Congressman Kendrik Meeks (D-FL) arrived overnight in 
Haiti. (TF1/Interagency Conference Call) 
 
¶3. (SBU) Paul Farmer, one of the founders of Partners in 
Health, and Leslie Voltaire, Deputy UN Special Envoy to Haiti, 
plan to travel to Haiti January 17.  SOUTHCOM is responding to 
Haitian Ambassador to the United States Joseph's request for a 
priority landing slot. (TF1/Embassy Port-au-Prince email) 
 
SEARCH AND RESCUE (SAR) 
---------------------- 
 
¶4. (U) As of 1100 EST, U.S. SAR had rescued 26 individuals, 
including three from the Caribbean Market. (TF1/OFDA Email) 
 
¶5. (SBU) SAR operations continue at a daycare center, a 
university, Hotel Christopher, and the Caribbean Market.  SAR 
will continue as long as there are signs of life. 
(TF1/Interagency Conference Call) 
 
COMMODITY FLOW 
-------------- 
 
¶6. (SBU) Commodities were distributed overnight from 
distribution sites, but security and transportation remain the 
key challenges at the 14 identified sites. (TF1/Interagency 
Conference Call) 
 
¶7. (U) OFDA has six additional water purification units en 
route to Haiti.  Twelve thousand water containers have arrived 
in Port-au-Prince. (TF1/OFDA e-mail) 
 
AMERICAN CITIZEN WELFARE/WHEREABOUTS/EVACUATIONS 
--------------------------------------------- ------------ 
 
¶8. (SBU) 139 Americans departed Port-au-Prince on flights the 
morning of January 17.  2086 total Americans have been 
evacuated to date. (TF1/Embassy Port-au-Prince e-mail) 
 
¶9. (SBU) 50 Americans are currently at the airport; 320 
Americans are at the embassy; 260 others are outside the 
embassy being screened and validated. (TF1/Interagency 
Conference Call) 
 
¶10. (SBU) Consular Affairs is coordinating with U.S. Embassies 
Santo Domingo and Port-au-Prince to provide TDY consular 
support.  Embassy Santo Domingo is exploring chartering buses 
to transport Americans from Port-au-Prince for evacuation from 
Santo Domingo. 
(TF1/CA e-mail) 
 
¶11. (U) Confirmed private American citizen deaths remain at 
¶15. (TF1/Embassy Port-au-Prince e-mail) 
 
MEDICAL RESPONSE 
---------------- 
 
¶12. (SBU) Embassy Port-au-Prince is working with SOUTHCOM to 
transport a Disaster Medical Assistance Team (DMAT) to the 
central hospital in Port-au-Prince, which reports 1500 
patients and limited medical supplies and security challenges. 
(TF1/Interagency Conference Call) 
 
¶13. (SBU) Sanitation conditions are deteriorating quickly; 
health and sanitation kits were prioritized and are scheduled 
to arrive January 17. (TF1/Interagency Conference Call) 
 
INTERNATIONAL COORDINATION/REQUESTS 
----------------------------------- 
 
¶14. (U) UNSYG Ban identified three priorities: saving as many 
lives as possible, stepping up humanitarian aid, and 
coordinating the huge amount of aid headed to Haiti. (AP) 
 
DEPLOYMENT OF U.S. GOVERNMENT ASSETS 
------------------------------------ 
 
¶15. (SBU) Frigate USS Underwood is underway and expected to 
arrive in Haiti January 17. (TF1/SOUTHCOM telcon) 
 
¶16. (SBU) 500 additional troops from the 82nd Airborne will 
arrive today, taking U.S. military personnel on the ground to 
1,300; total U.S. military personnel in the area of 
operations, including offshore, is 5,302.  Troop levels on the 
ground are expected to reach 3,300 by January 19. 
(TF1/SOUTHCOM telcon) 
 
¶17. (SBU) The Loomis crane ship is expected to arrive in Port- 
au-Prince January 22.  Two additional Maritime Administration 
crane ships have been deployed; two private crane ships also 
may be contracted. (TF1/Interagency Conference Call) 
 
HUMANITARIAN/SECURITY/INFRASTRUCTURE 
------------------------------------ 
 
¶18. (SBU) 100 flights will arrive in Port-au-Prince January 
¶17. 
(TF1/Interagency Conference Call) 
 
¶19. (SBU) SOUTHCOM is completing its assessment of San Isidro 
Airbase in the Dominican Republic, hoping to make this the 
primary route for aid delivery for onward transport overland 
to Haiti. (TF1/Interagency Conference Call) 
 
¶20. (SBU) There are reports of only sporadic violence and no 
significant deterioration of security. (TF1/Interagency 
Conference Call) 
 
¶21. (SBU) USAID's Office of Transition Initiatives is 
conducting an assessment to help stand up Haitian government 
ministries. (TF1/OFDA telcon) 
 
¶22. (SBU) Cell phone service has improved, and Post estimates 
the principal cellular network is currently operating at 50 
percent capacity.  (TF1/Interagency Conference Call) 
 
¶23. (SBU) President Preval welcomed the U.S. Government's 
offer to facilitate the transfer of reserve funds to Haiti's 
Central Bank; the U.S. Department of Treasury is exploring 
transferring up to $100 million. (TF1/Interagency Conference 
Call)
