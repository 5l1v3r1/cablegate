¶1. This is an Action Request.  See paragraphs 2-4. 
 
¶2. The Department's Office of Defense Trade Controls 
Compliance (PM/DTCC) requests Embassy's assistance in 
conducting a post-shipment check on license 050101261 for the 
export of military radios to Poland.  PM/DDTC outreach team 
plans to complete this check in conjunction with Embassy 
during its visit to Poland on September 10 and 11. 
 
¶3. Reason for request:  WB Electronics, unfamiliar foreign 
consignee. 
 
¶4. ACTION: Embassy and PM/DDTC team is requested to make 
inquiries with appropriate Polish Army and WB Electronics 
officials to determine if it received 108 units shipped from 
the U.S. on July 24, 2008 and that radios are being used for 
purpose stated on license.  Polish Army POC is M. Yzewczyk; 
reference DSP-83 Nontransfer and Use Certificate signed on 
April 4, 2008.  Department requests that team conduct a site 
visit to WB Electronics in order to assess its business 
operations, on-site security, and general reliability as a 
recipient of United States Munitions List (USML) items.  POC 
is Piotr Wojciechowski, Chairman, TEL: 48-22-731-25-51; 
reference Purchase Order # 84v3/2008 signed on April 17, 
¶2008.  The following questions are offered as suggested 
guidance with WB Electronics: 
 
- Can the company provide documentation from the Polish Army 
(e.g. purchase order, contract, or letter of intent) 
establishing it as the final end user of these items? 
 
- Does the company understand the restrictions on United 
States Munitions List (USML) articles, particularly regarding 
re-export and retransfer? 
 
- What is the company's exact role in this transaction? 
 
- Who are the company's main clients? 
 
End Action.
