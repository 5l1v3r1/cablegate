SUBJECT: LAUNCH OF SPACE SHUTTLE MISSION STS-125 
 
¶1.  SPACE SHUTTLE MISSION STS-125, ATLANTIS (OV-104), 
WILL LAUNCH FROM KENNEDY SPACE CENTER (KSC), FLORIDA, NO 
EARLIER THAN MAY 11, 2009.  THE LAUNCH WINDOW OPENS AT 
13:42 EDT, (17:42 UTC/GMT) AND IS 62 MINUTES LONG. 
NOMINAL END OF MISSION LANDING AT KSC IS SCHEDULED FOR 
NO EARLIER THAN MAY 22, 2009 AT 11:42 EDT (15:42 
UTC/GMT). 
 
¶2.  MORON AIR BASE, SPAIN IS THE PRIMARY TRANSOCEANIC 
ABORT LANDING SITE (TAL).  THERE ARE NO ALTERNATE TAL 
SITES FOR THIS MISSION. 
 
¶3.  OES/SAT WILL STAFF THE JOINT STATE-NASA SHUTTLE 
MONITORING GROUP IN THE DEPARTMENT'S OPERATIONS CENTER 
FOR THE LIFT-OFF PERIOD.  THE OPERATIONS CENTER WILL 
ESTABLISH A CONFERENCE CALL TELEPHONE LINK BETWEEN THE 
MONITORING GROUP AND SHUTTLE CONTROL OFFICERS AT EMBASSY 
MADRID AND EMBASSY PARIS, AT APPROX. ONE HALF HOUR PRIOR 
TO LIFT-OFF.  SHUTTLE MONITORING GROUP CONTROL OFFICERS 
ARE OES/SAT, DORIETHA JACKSON AND NEEVY VAN LANINGHAM, 
AND SHARI KAMM, NASA.
