SUBJ:  ZIMBABWE:  1992 ANNUAL TERRORISM REPORT 
 
REF:   A. STATE 338430 (NOTAL), B. HARARE 5889 (NOTAL), 
       ¶C. HARARE 5768 (NOTAL), D. 91 HARARE 10989 (NOTAL 
 
¶1. THE FOLLOWING INFORMATION IS KEYED TO REF A: 
 
 
¶A. PROSECUTIONS INVOLVING INTERNATIONAL TERRORISM 
   --------------------------------------------- - 
   AND/OR SIGNIFICANT ACTS OF DOMESTIC TERRORISM 
   --------------------------------------------- 
 
¶2. THERE WERE NO PROSECUTIONS INVOLVING INTERNATIONAL OR 
DOMESTIC TERRORISM IN 1992.  ONLY TWO TERRORIST 
INCIDENTS OCCURRED IN 1992--THE MAY 24 BOMBING OF A 
BULAWAYO HOUSE USED BY THE AFRICAN NATIONAL CONGRESS 
(ANC) IN WHICH NO ONE WAS INJURED (REP C) AND A 
CROSS-BORDER RAID BY THE MOZAMBICAN NATIONAL RESISTANCE 
(MNR OR RENAMO) THAT SAME MONTH IN WHICH A NUMBER OF 
ZIMBABWEANS WERE ASSAULTED (REP B). 
 
 
¶B. EXTRADITION OF SUSPECTED TERRORISTS 
   ----------------------------------- 
 
¶3. ZIMBABWE NEITHER EXTRADITED NOR REQUESTED EXTRADITION 
OF SUSPECTED TERRORISTS FOR PROSECUTION. 
 
 
¶C. SIGNIFICANT IMPEDIMENTS TO PROSECUTION AND EXTRADITI 
   --------------------------------------------- ------- 
 
¶4. THERE ARE NO SIGNIFICANT IMPEDIMENTS TO PROSECUTION 
OF SUSPECTED TERRORISTS.  HOWEVER, ZIMBABWE DOES NOT 
HAVE EXTRADITION TREATIES WITH MOST NON-COMMONWEALTH 
COUNTRIES. 
 
 
¶D. RESPONSE TO TERRORISM 
   --------------------- 
 
¶5. ZIMBABWEAN OFFICIALS DID NOT MAKE ANY PUBLIC 
STATEMENTS ABOUT TERRORISM IN 1992.  THE JULY 20, 1991, 
BOMBING OF THE HARARE SHERATON HOTEL AND THE MAY 24 
BOMBING REMAIN UNSOLVED.  FOUR SOUTH AFRICAN AGENTS 
CONVICTED OF TERRORIST ACTS IN THE 1980'S REMAIN ON 
DEATH ROW. 
 
 
¶E. MAJOR COUNTERTERRORISM EFFORTS 
   ------------------------------ 
 
¶6. IN ADDITION TO DEPLOYING UNIFORMED POLICE OUTSIDE TH 
U.S. EMBASSY, ZIMBABWE CONTINUED TO MAINTAIN A 
SIGNIFICANT MILITARY AND POLICE PRESENCE ALONG THE 
MOZAMBICAN BORDER TO DETER INCURSIONS BY RENAMO. 
 
 
¶F. SUPPORT FOR INTERNATIONAL TERRORISM 
   ----------------------------------- 
 
¶7. ZIMBABWE HAS FULL DIPLOMATIC RELATIONS WITH CUBA, 
IRAN, IRAQ, LIBYA, NORTH KOREA, AND THE SO-CALLED "STAT 
OF PALESTINE."  ALL MAINTAIN EMBASSIES IN HARARE, AND 
THE PALESTINIAN LIBERATION ORGANIZATION'S (PLO'S) 
REPRESENTATIVE IS THE DEAN OF THE DIPLOMATIC CORPS.  IN 
INTERNATIONAL FORA ZIMBABWE HAS CONDEMNED TERRORISM IN 
GENERAL. 
 
 
¶G. SUPPORT OF TERRORIST SUPPORTING COUNTRIES 
   ----------------------------------------- 
 
¶8. ZIMBABWE HAS NOT MADE ANY PUBLIC STATEMENTS IN 
SUPPORT OF A TERRORIST SUPPORTING COUNTRY ON A TERRORIS 
ISSUE. 
 
 
¶H. SIGNIFICANT CHANGE IN ATTITUDE TOWARD TERRORISM 
   --------------------------------------------- -- 
 
¶9. THERE HAS NO SIGNIFICANT CHANGE IN ZIMBABWE'S 
ATTITUDE TOWARD TERRORISM IN 1992.  RELATIONS WITH CUBA 
IRAN, IRAQ, AND LIBYA HAVE BEEN COOL, WITH 
IRANIAN-ZIMBABWEAN RELATIONS DETERIORATING BECAUSE OF 
IRANIAN EFFORTS TO BRING ISLAMIC FUNDAMENTALISM TO 
ZIMBABWE. 
 
 
¶I. COMPLIANCE WITH UNSC RESOLUTIONS ON LIBYA 
   ----------------------------------------- 
 
¶10. ZIMBABWE HAS NOT COMPLIED WITH UNITED NATIONS 
SECURITY COUNCIL RESOLUTIONS ON LIBYA.  VICE PRESIDENT 
JOSHUA NKOMO AND MINISTER OF FOREIGN AFFAIRS NATHAN 
SHAMUYARIRA HAVE VISITED LIBYA, AND TWO LIBYAN MINISTER 
HAVE VISITED ZIMBABWE SINCE THE RESOLUTIONS WERE 
PASSED.  ZIMBABWE HAS NOT EXPELLED ANY LIBYAN DIPLOMATS 
ALTHOUGH FIVE LIBYANS, INCLUDING ONE LIBYAN DIPLOMAT, 
DEPARTED VOLUNTARILY IN 1992.
