SUBJECT: SUDAN:  MFA SAYS NO GUESTS, HIGH-LEVEL VISITS 
DURING RAMADAN 
 
REF: KHARTOUM 02408 
 
¶1. (U) On October 1, Embassy Khartoum received a diplomatic 
note from the Ministry of Foreign Affairs dated September 30 
regarding guests and visitors to Sudan. 
 
¶2. (U) The text of the dip note (received in Arabic ) 
unofficial Embassy translation) is as follows: 
 
(Begin text) 
 
The Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the Republic of the Sudan 
presents its compliments to all diplomatic missions and 
international organizations accredited to the Sudan and would 
like to advise that on the occasion of the Holy Month of 
Ramadan, Sudanese officials will not be able to receive any 
guests during this great month.  Kindly schedule the visits 
of ministers and senior officials to the Sudan after the 
great month of Ramadan. 
 
The Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the Republic of the Sudan 
avails itself of this opportunity to renew to all diplomatic 
missions and international and regional organizations 
accredited to the Sudan the assurances of its highest 
consideration. 
 
(End text) 
 
¶3. (SBU) Comment:  Reftel details an additional MFA dip note 
limiting the movement of U.S. citizens and officials in what 
appears to be a concerted effort to exert strict limitations 
on the activities of U.S. nationals in Sudan.
