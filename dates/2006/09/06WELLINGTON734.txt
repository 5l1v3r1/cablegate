SUBJECT:  NEW ZEALAND WILL GIVE CAREFUL CONSIDERATION TO U.S. 
CANDIDATE FOR INTERNATIONAL LAW COMMISSION 
 
REF:  State 137429 
 
¶1.  Embassy delivered reftel diplomatic note and talking points 
regarding Michael Matheson's re-election to the International Law 
Commission to New Zealand's Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Trade 
(MFAT) on 23 August.  Embassy is in receipt of a response note from 
MFAT.  The text of the note follows: 
 
¶2.  Begin text: 
 
"The Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Trade presents its compliments 
to the Embassy of the United States of America and has the honour to 
refer to the Embassy's Note No. 53-06 of 23 August 2006 informing 
the Ministry that the United States has nominated Michael Matheson 
for re-election to the International Law Commission at the elections 
to be held during the 61st United Nations General Assembly. 
 
The Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Trade wishes to assure the 
Embassy of the United States of America that New Zealand will give 
careful consideration to this candidature. 
 
The Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Trade takes this opportunity to 
renew to the Embassy of the United States of America the assurances 
of its highest consideration." 
 
McCormick
