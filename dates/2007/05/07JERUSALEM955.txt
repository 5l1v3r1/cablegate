SUBJECT: GAZA: HAMAS-FATAH TENSION SIMMERS, AS QASSAMS AND 
ISRAELI AIRSTRIKES CONTINUE 
 
REF: JERUSALEM 940 AND PREVIOUS 
 
¶1.  (SBU) Summary.  Interfactional violence remained at a low 
level May 21-22, but Hamas-Fatah shootings left three 
injured, and deaths from injuries in previous fighting raised 
the toll from factional clashes to 56.  20 Qassam rockets 
struck Israel from 1400 (local) May 21 to 1600 May 22, 
killing one Isaeli and injuring two in Sderot and causing 
subsantial property damage.  The IAF hit three 
metalblacksmith shops in Gaza City late May 21 and earlyMay 
22 and heavily damaged an Executive Force HQ injuring five 
Palestinians.  End summary. 
 
Fational Shootings/Abductions 
------------------------------ 
 
¶2.  (SBU) While the ceasefire generaly held, there were 
several violations May 21-22. At 1300 (local) May 22, Hamas 
military-wing gunen abducted and shot Preventive Security 
(PSO) oficer and Fatah activist Muhammad Shaldan in Gaza 
City.  On May 21, unknown gunmen shot and injuredtwo 
Palestinians with Hamas ties in Khan Yunis.   Hamas military 
wing member, a Fatah fighter, an a non-combatant Palestinian 
shot in factional cashes died of injuries previously 
sustained, raiing the total number of Palestinian killed in 
Faah-Hamas fighting to 56. 
 
Qassams and Israeli Airtrikes Continue 
-------------------------------------- 
 
¶3.  (SBU) 20 Qassam rockets struck Israe from 1400 (local) 
May 21 to 1600 May 22, killin one Israeli and injuring two 
(one seriously) in Sderot and causing substantial property 
damage to commercial buildings, vehicles and an industrial 
area.  Rockets hit Sderot, Be'eri, Kfar Azza and south of 
Ashkelon.  Hamas claimed 14 rocket launches and 17 mortar 
firings during this period, and the Popular Resistance 
Committees (PRCs) and PIJ both claimed launches at 2000 local 
when the Israeli woman was killed. 
 
¶4.  (SBU) Palestinian media and security contacts report the 
IAF destroyed three metal/blacksmith shops in Gaza City late 
May 21 and early May 22.  Palestinian newspapers report these 
attacks injured at least five Palestinians.  At 1330 local, 
an IAF strike heavily damaged an Executive Force HQ.  A Hamas 
spokesman told Palestinian press that the Executive Force had 
evacuated the site beforehand and no injuries resulted. 
 
 
Comment 
------- 
 
¶5.  (SBU) Hamas and Fatah are generally observing the 
interfactional ceasefire while Israeli airstrikes and 
shelling are underway.  However, recent violations 
demonstrate that Fatah-Hamas tension remains high and suggest 
the May 19 ceasefire may not last.
