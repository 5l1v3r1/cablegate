VIP TRAVEL 
---------- 
 
1.(SBU) No updates at this time. 
 
SEARCH AND RESCUE 
----------------- 
 
¶2. (SBU) Prime Minister Bellerive said he is concerned about 
the lack of SAR operations outside of Port-au-Prince, the 
possibility of epidemics, and the perception rescue efforts 
are focused on foreigners rather than Haitians. 
(TF1/Embassy Port-Au-Prince telcon) 
 
¶3. (SBU) Seven Americans were rescued from the Hotel Montana. 
(TF1/Interagency Conference Call) 
 
¶4.  (SBU) Fifteen SAR teams are in Haiti; six additional teams 
are standing by to deploy. UNDAC reports no new teams are 
needed due to lack of in-country support. Chances of survival 
are drastically reduced after 72 hours, approximately 1700 EST 
January 15. (TF1/Interagency Conference Call) 
 
COMMODITY FLOW 
-------------- 
 
¶5.  (SBU) Shipments from Homestead AFB have begun.  Several 
flights have been planned over the next ten days, and 
coordination is in progress on distribution sites and flight 
clearances. (TF1/Interagency Conference Call) 
 
AMERICAN CITIZEN WELFARE/WHEREABOUTS/EVACUATIONS 
--------------------------------------------- ------------ 
 
¶6.  (SBU) Media report more than 160 Americans at the 
airport.  Post notes it is difficult to estimate the exact 
number.  As of 0900, approximately 200 people were awaiting 
evacuation at the Embassy.  Numbers are expected to increase. 
A four-person consular team is at the airport. 
  (TF1/Interagency Conference Call, AP) 
 
¶7.  (SBU) Consular teams will assist at Miami Airport and 
Homestead AFB. (TF1/Embassy Miami Passport Office e-mail) 
 
¶8.  (SBU) Florida Governor Crist requested a USAID liaison for 
Florida's Emergency Operations Center in anticipation of a 
mass migration of refugees. (TF1/FEMA e-mail) 
 
¶9.  (SBU) Legislative Affairs is responding to 700 
Congressional inquiries. (TF1/H e-mail) 
 
MEDICAL RESPONSE 
---------------- 
 
¶10.  (SBU) Under a new agreement between Haiti and the United 
States, U.S. physicians will be allowed to treat quake 
victims. (TF1/Embassy Port-Au-Prince telcon) 
 
¶11.  (U) Experts from the Pan American Health Organization 
 
(PAHO) and World Health Organization (WHO) Regional Disaster 
Response Team are establishing a field office in Jimani, 
Dominican Republic, near the Haitian border and will work to 
distribute supplies to health centers.  Warehouses in the 
Dominican Republic lack space for supplies and donations. 
(TF1/WHO/PAHO Situation Report) 
 
¶12.  (SBU) Thirteen Western Hemisphere countries have deployed 
medical teams. (TF1/WHO/PAHO Situation Report) 
 
INTERNATIONAL COORDINATION/REQUESTS 
----------------------------------- 
 
¶13.  (SBU) Senior UN officials Boldue and Mulet have taken 
charge of MINUSTAH, which expects to deliver 60,000 meals/day. 
The UN will launch its flash appeal for Haiti at 1600 EST. 
(TF1/Embassy telcon, TF1/IO e-mail) 
 
DEPLOYMENT OF U.S. GOVERNMENT ASSETS 
------------------------------------ 
 
¶14.  (SBU) Seven Diplomatic Security personnel are en route to 
Port-au-Prince from Santo Domingo.  (TF1/DS) 
 
¶15.  (SBU) Approximately 600 military personnel are on the 
ground to provide humanitarian assistance and support 
logistics.  (TF1/Interagency Conference Call) 
 
¶16.  (SBU) Approximately 4,000 U.S. military personnel will be 
in Haiti by January 16 and 10,000 personnel by January 18. 
(TF1/DoD telcon) 
 
¶17.  (SBU) USS Carl Vinson arrived and is conducting 
operations. (TF1/SOUTHCOM) 
 
HUMANITARIAN/SECURITY/INFRASTRUCTURE 
------------------------------------ 
 
¶18.  (SBU) Haiti granted airport control to the United 
States.  Airport capacity could reach 90 planes per day. 
(TF1/Embassy Port-Au-Prince telcon, TF1/Interagency Conference 
Call ) 
 
¶19.  (SBU) Embassy expects gas stations to run out of fuel 
January 15 and food supplies in stores to be depleted January 
18 or 19. (TF1/Embassy Port-Au-Prince telcon) 
 
¶20.  (SBU) Media report 4,000 escaped inmates are roaming the 
streets.  The Embassy comments there is no Haitian police 
presence in Port-au-Prince and there are increasing reports of 
looting and other security problems. MINUSTAH is patrolling 
the streets. 
 (TF1 /Embassy telcon, TF1/Interagency Conference Call, AP) 
 
¶21.  (SBU) Looting of UN food supplies in Port-au-Prince was 
limited, media report.  A spokesperson said the UN would soon 
distribute 6,000 tons of food, 40 percent of pre-quake stocks 
in Haiti. (TF1/Associated Press) 
 
¶22.  (SBU) Haitian cellular network Voila is operating at 70 
percent. (TF1/EEB e-mail) 
 
POLITICAL DEVELOPMENTS 
---------------------- 
 
¶23.  (SBU) Former Haitian President Aristide said he wants to 
return to Haiti to help with the catastrophe, but did not 
indicate concrete plans. (AP)
