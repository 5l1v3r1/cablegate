SUBJECT: "JEUNE AFRIQUE" JUNE 19-25 SUPPLEMENT ON 
"US/AFRICA: SHARED INTERESTS" 
 
¶1. PARIS-BASED INFLUENTIAL PANAFRICAN WEEKLY "JEUNE AFRIQUE- 
L'INTELLIGENT" DATED JUNE 19-26 HAS A 16-PAGE SUPPLEMENT ON 
US/AFRICA RELATIONS UNDER GLOBAL TITLE "SHARED INTERESTS". LIST 
OF ARTICLES FOLLOWS: 
 
  -    "GOOD GUYS" (TITLE IN ENGLISH), EDITORIAL BY MAGAZINE 
     ASSISTANT DIRECTOR FRANCOIS SOUDAN ON STATE OF PLAY BETWEEN THE 
     BUSH ADMINISTRATION AND (FAVORED) AFRICAN HEADS OF STATE. 
-    "NEW FRONTIERS", BY ELISE COLETTE: "AIDS, ENVIRONMENT AND 
OIL DRILLING ARE WASHINTON'S PRIORITIES IN AFRICA. HOWEVER, SINCE 
9/11, PREVENTION AGAINST TERRORIST ATTACKS HAS GIVEN A NEW 
DIMENSION TO US/AFRICA RELATIONS". 
-    "STEPHEN HAYES'PRIVATE THOUGHTS", INTERVIEW WITH CORPORATE 
COUNCIL ON AFRICA'S CHAIRMAN BY ZIAD LIMAM. 
-    "CHARITY AND BUSINESS", BY PHILIPPE PERDRIX. "MARKET ECONOMY 
ON ONE HAND, COMPASSION ON THE OTHER, ARE AT THE ROOT OF THE BUSH 
ADMINISTRATION'S AFRICAN POLICY". 
-    "NGOs ON THE FRONT LINE", BY PHILIPPE PERDRIX. "HEALTH, FOOD 
AID, ENVIRONMENT, HUMAN RIGHTS: NGOs ARE EVERYWHERE ON THE 
GROUND". 
-    "FROM HIV/AIDS TO TERRORISM", BY JEAN DOMINIQUE GESLIN, 
LISTING MAIN FIELDS OF AMERICAN INTERVENTION: AIDS, MILLENIUM 
ACCOUNT, PEACE KEEPING, SECURITY, DEBT, TRADE, FOOD AND 
AGRICULTURE, EDUCATION, WATER, ENVIRONMENT. 
-    "THE BLACK GOLD RUSH", BY PASCAL AIRAULT. "TO DIVERSIFY ITS 
SOURCES OF SUPPLY, UNCLE SAM COUNTS ON THE GULF OF GUINEA 
OILFIELDS. BY 2015, A QUARTER OF ITS IMPORTS WILL COME FROM 
AFRICAN PRODUCERS". 
-    "TO PREVENT AND TO CURE", CONCLUDING EDITORIAL BY CHERIF 
OUAZANI (FORMER ARS IV). 
 
¶2. PLEASE NOTE: ALL ARTICLES ARE KEPT FOR A MONTH AND CAN BE 
FAXED OR POSTED AT POSTS' REQUEST. SEND REQUESTS BY E-MAIL TO 
BROOKESA@STATE.GOV
