SUBJECT: POPE WELCOMES IRAQ'S APPARENT OPENNESS TO NEW ARMS 
INSPECTIONS 
 
 
¶1.   POPE JOHN PAUL II ENDED HIS WEEKLY GENERAL AUDIENCE 
SEPTEMBER 18 BY WELCOMING IRAQ'S APPARENT ACCEPTANCE OF UN 
ARMS INSPECTIONS AS POTENTIALLY A STEP BACK FROM WAR.  THE 
VATICAN'S PRESS OFFICE SLUGGED THE PAPAL STATEMENT IN ITS 
DAILY INFORMATION BULLETIN AS "POPE MAKES APPEAL FOR PEACE." 
TEXT OF THE POPE'S STATEMENT FOLLOWS: 
 
                       UNCLASSIFIED 
 
PAGE 02        VATICA  04539  190621Z 
¶2.   BEGIN TEXT.  "IN THE LAST FEW DAYS, AFTER THE WINDS OF 
WAR THREATENED TO UPSET THE ENTIRE REGION OF THE MIDDLE 
EAST, I HAVE RECEIVED THE GOOD NEWS OF THE POSSIBILITY OF 
IRAQ'S COLLABORATION ONCE AGAIN WITH THE INTERNATIONAL 
COMMUNITY.  I URGE YOU TO CONTINUE TO PRAY THAT THE LORD 
ENLIGHTENS THE LEADERS OF NATIONS, OPENS US AND SUSTAINS 
ATTITUDES OF GOOD WILL AND LEADS HUMANITY, AFFLICTED BY SO 
MANY EVILS, TOWARD COEXISTENCE FREE FROM WAR AND VIOLENCE." 
END TEXT.
