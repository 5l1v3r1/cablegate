REF: A. MANAGUA 448 
     ¶B. MANAGUA 207 
 
¶1.  On May 5 we received a letter to the Ambassador signed by 
Manuel Coronel Kautz, Vice-Minister of Foreign Affairs, 
requesting a meeting with WHA/CEN Director John Feeley to 
discuss Nicaragua's Merida Initiative Counter Proposal during 
the upcoming June 13 meeting of the Central American Security 
Commission (SICA) in El Salvador, where the Nicaraguans plan 
to present their proposal.  An unofficial translation of the 
letter follows.  Original letter in Spanish has been passed 
to WHA/CEN. 
 
¶2.  BEGIN TEXT OF DIP CORRESPONDENCE: 
 
Vice-Minister of Foreign Affairs 
Managua, 30 April 2008 
MRE/DVM/0299/04/008 
 
Mr. Paul A. Trivelli 
Ambassador of the United States of America 
His Office 
 
cc: Mr. Samuel Santos L., Minister 
 
Dear Mr. Ambassador: 
 
I am writing to you regarding the upcoming meeting of the 
Central American Security Commission, as well as the visit of 
WHA/CEN Director Mr. John Feeley. 
 
On this matter, following upon our discussion of March 25 at 
the Foreign Minister's office, as well as our request to 
Deputy Assistant Secretary Thomas Shannon on March 7 in 
Washington, we remind you about the need to arrange a meeting 
with Mr. John Feeley to discuss Nicaragua's Proposal of 
funding that will be presented at the upcoming Security 
Commission meeting. 
 
I thank you for your attention to this letter, and I take the 
opportunity to greet you with the assurances of my 
consideration. 
 
Sincerely, 
/s/ Manuel Coronel Kautz 
 
END TEXT OF DIPLOMATIC NOTE 
 
¶3.  Post will forward signed original to WHA/CEN via 
diplomatic pouch.
