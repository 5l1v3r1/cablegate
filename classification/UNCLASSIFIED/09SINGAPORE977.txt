SUBJECT: SINGAPORE OFFERS DISASTER RELIEF TO INDONESIA AND 
VIETNAM 
 
¶1. (U)  Summary:  GOS has offered humanitarian assistance and 
disaster relief aid to Indonesia, Vietnam, Philippines, 
Cambodia, and Laos in the wake of the Padang Earthquake and 
Typhoon Ketsana.  Assistance offers range from military 
aircraft assistance and deployment of elite Singapore Civil 
Defence Force search and rescue and medical personnel,to 
government donations to the Singapore Red Cross to kickstart 
private giving.  End Summary. 
 
 
SINGAPORE'S OFFER OF DISASTER RELIEF TO INDONESIA 
 
 
¶2.  (U)  Singapore is providing US$50K worth of emergency 
relief supplies, including temporary shelters, blankets, and 
medicines, to Indonesia.  On Oct 2, Singapore dispatched two 
Singapore Air Force (SAF) C-130 with supplies and a 42-member 
Singapore Civil Defence Force (SCDF) rescue contingent from 
SCDF's elite Disaster Assistance & Rescue Team (DART), to 
assist the Indonesians in their search and rescue operations. 
 On Oct 3, the SAF sent four C-130's with 54 SAF and Ministry 
of Health medical personnel.  Personnel proceeded to 
Pariaman, 50 km north of Pandang and close to the epicenter, 
to set up a medical facility. 
 
 
SINGAPORE OFFERS DISASTER RELIEF TO PHILIPPINES, VIETNAM, 
CAMBODIA, AND LAOS 
 
 
¶3.  (U)  GOS announced a US$20K donation to the Singapore Red 
Cross (SRC) to kickstart its appeal for donations for those 
affected by Typhoon Ketsana in the Philippines.  Singapore 
dispatched US$30K worth of humanitarian relief supplies to 
the Philippines.  GOS announced it will contriute another 
US$50K as seed money to kickstart the SRC joint appeal drive 
for donations for those countries in the region affected by 
Typhoon Ketsana, including Vietnam, Cambodia, and Laos.
