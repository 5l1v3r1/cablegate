¶2.   SPANISH DAILY PRESS OF OCTOBER 19, 2004 REPORTED 
  THAT THE SPANISH POLICE ARRESTED SEVERAL ISLAMIC 
  EXTREMISTS WHO WERE BELIEVE TO BE PLANNING SUICIDE 
  ATTACKS WITH EXPLOSIVES AGAINST THE SPANISH NATIONAL 
  HIGH COURT AND OTHER SIMBOLIC BUILDINGS IN MADRID.  THE 
  ARRESTS TOOK PLACE IN DIFFERENT LOCATIONS IN SPAIN. 
  ACCORDING TO THE INVESTIGATION, THEY WERE RECRUITED AND 
  LEADERED BY MOHAMED ACHRAF, WHO WAS ARRESTED IN 
  SWITZERLAND ON AUG. 28, 2004, AT THE REQUEST OF SPANISH 
  AUTHORITIES. THE MAJORITY OF THE TERRORIST ISLAMIST 
  CELL MEMBERS ARRESTED, INCLUDING ACHRAF, MET DURING 
  2001 AND 2002, IN THE SPANISH PENITENTIARY OF TOPAS IN 
  SALAMANCA SPAIN, WHERE SOME WERE SERVING SENTENCES FOR 
  CRIMINAL RELATED ISSUES AND OTHERS FOR TERRORIST 
  ASSOCIATION AND MEMBERSHIP IN THE ALGERIAN TERRORIST 
  ORGANIZATION GIA AND/OR ITS RADICAL ARMS THE "SALAFIST 
  GROUP FOR THE PREACH AND COMBAT" AND "TAKFIR WAQ 
  HIJRA".  DURING THIS TIME, ACHRAF MANAGED TO INFLUENCE 
  THE ISLAMISTS TOWARDS RADICAL POSITIONS AND THE 
  PRACTICE OF THE "JIHAD".  ALTHOUGH THEY WERE DISPERSED 
  AMONG OTHER PRISONS, THEY LATELY MAINTAINED A FLUID 
  CORRESPONDENCE AND CLOSE CONTACT BETWEEN THEM AND ALSO 
  WITH OTHER OUTSIDER RADICAL EXTREMISTS AND TERRORIST 
  SUSPECTS.
