PRESIDENT DEBY GETS INVESTIGATIVE COMMISSION REPORT 
--------------------------------------------- -------- 
 
¶1.  INVESTIGATION INTO THE JANUARY AND FEBRUARY 2008 EVENTS: 
PRESIDENT DEBY SAYS HE WILL "FOLLOW UP" RECOMMENDATIONS OF THE 
REPORT. THE COMMISSION INVESTIGATING THE EVENTS OF JANUARY AND 
FEBRUARY 2008 SUBMITTED ITS REPORT TO PRESIDENT DEBY ON TUESDAY, 
AUGUST 5. IN RECEIVING THE REPORT, PRESIDENT DEBY DECLARED THAT HE 
WILL STRICTLY FOLLOW THE RECOMMENDATIONS CONTAINED IN THE DOCUMENT, 
AND THAT THE GOVERNMENT WILL "CAREFULLY FOLLOW IT UP." NO INDICATION 
WAS GIVEN AS TO THE DATE WHEN THE 160-PAGE REPORT WOULD BE RELEASED 
TO THE PUBLIC. IN RECEIVING THE REPORT, PRESIDENT DEBY TOLD THE HEAD 
OF THE COMMISSION, MR. ALLAISSEM DJAIBE, THAT THE GOC WOULD 
ESTABLISH A MINISTERIAL-LEVEL FOLLOW-UP COMMITTEE.  (SEE PARA 11 
BELOW.)

CHINESE FIND OIL DEPOSIT IN SOUTHWESTERN CHAD 
--------------------------------------------- -- 
 
¶2.  HIGH-QUALITY OIL DEPOSIT DISCOVERED IN THE BONGOR AREA.   THE 
MINISTER OF OIL, MR. MAHAMAT NASSER HASSANE, ANNOUNCED ON AUGUST 13 
THAT A DEPOSIT OF HIGH-QUALITY PETROLEUM HAS BEEN DISCOVERED IN 
SOUTHWESTERN CHAD NEAR THE TOWN OF BONGOR BY THE CHINESE NATIONAL 
PETROLEUM COMPANY (CNPC). HE DECLARED THAT, ONCE EXPLOITED, THIS OIL 
WOULD BE REFINED AT A REFINERY TO BE LOCATED IN DJERMAYA ON THE 
OUTSKIRTS OF NDJAMENA IN ORDER TO SATISFY INTERNAL DEMAND.

HIGH COURT CONDEMNS REBEL CHIEFS, HABRE 
---------------------------------------- 
 
¶3.  HIGH COURT CONDEMNS REBELS, EX-PRESIDENT HABRE IN ABSENTIA. 
(AUGUST 13-15):  DURING ITS FIRST SESSION AUGUST 12-15, THE CRIMINAL 
COURT PRONOUNCED HARSH SENTENCES AGAINST CURRENT CHAD REBEL LEADERS 
AS WELL AS FORMER PRESIDENT HISSEIN HABRE.  TWELVE REBEL LEADERS AND 
HABRE WERE SENTENCED TO DEATH IN ABSTENTIA. AMONG THOSE SENTENCED TO 
DEATH WERE MAJOR REBEL LEADERS MAHAMAT NOURI, TOM ERDIMI, TIMAN 
ERDIMI, AHMAT HASSABALLAH SOUBIANE, AND ABDOUL-WAHID ABOUD.  LIFE 
SENTENCES WERE PRONOUNCED AGAINST FORTY-TWO OTHER REBELS. THE COURT 
ALSO ORDERED THE CONFISCATION OF THE PROPERTY OF THE CONDEMNED.  IN 
REACTION TO RUMORS THAT THE SENTENCING OF HISSEIN HABRE BY THE 
CHADIAN CRIMINAL COURT WOULD PUT AN END TO HIS TRIAL PROCESS 
UNDERWAY IN SENEGAL, THE MINISTER OF JUSTICE, JEAN ALINGUE BAWOYEU 
DECLARED ON AUGUST 15 THAT THE NDJAMENA CRIMINAL COURT'S DECISION 
DOES NOT AFFECT THE TRIAL PROCESS IN SENEGAL.  HE EXPLAINED THAT 
HISSEIN HABRE WAS CONVICTED IN CHAD FOR HIS SUPPORT TO THE CHADIAN 
REBELS, WHILE HIS TRIAL IN SENEGAL IS RELATED TO "CRIME AGAINST 
HUMANITY AND OTHER ATROCITIES."

MORE RFC REBELS RALLY TO GOC 
------------------------------ 
 
¶5.  ELEMENTS OF THE RFC REBEL GROUP AGREE TO END ARMED STRUGGLE. 
THE GOC ANNOUNCED AUGUST 18 THAT THE CHADIAN GOVERNEMENT AND 
ELEMENTS OF THE "RASSEMBLEMENT DES FORCES POUR LE CHANGEMENT" (RFC - 
RALLY OF FORCES FOR CHANGE), SIGNED A PROTOCOL AGREEMENT IN 
APPLICATION OF THE SIRTE ACCORD. THE AGREEMENT WAS SIGNED BY THE 
NATIONAL MEDIATOR REPRESENTING THE GOC AND THE RFC DEFENSE 
COMMISSIONER IN THE PRESENCE OF THE LIBYAN AMBASSADOR TO CHAD. THE 
TERMS OF THE AGREEMENT INCLUDE AN IMMEDIATE CEASE-FIRE AND ASSEMBLY 
OF THE RFC FIGHTERS TO BE FOLLOWED BY THEIR INTEGRATION INTO THE 
CHADIAN NATIONAL ARMY OR (FOR CIVILIANS) INTO THE CHADIAN CIVIL 
SERVICE. INTEGRATION IS TO OCCUR WITHIN TWO MONTHS FROM THE DATE OF 
THE SIGNING OF THE AGREEMENT. THE FIGHTERS WILL ASSEMBLE IN IRIBA, 
WHERE THEY WILL BE COUNTED.  ONCE THEIR NUMBER IS KNOWN, THE 
NATIONAL ASSEMBLY WILL INITIATE AN AMNESTY LAW FOR THOSE WHO HAD 
PREVIOUSLY BEEN SANCTIONED BY THE GOVERNMENT.

GOC MICR-FINANCING PROJECT LAUNCHED 
------------------------------------ 
 
¶6.  NATIONAL MICRO-FINANCE STRATEGY: AT LEAST 500,000 TO ACCESS 
LOANS BETWEEN NOW AND 2013. DURING THE OPENING CEREMONY OF A SEMINAR 
ON THE NATIONAL MICRO-FINANCE STRATEGY ON AUGUST 18, THE 
VICE-MINISTER FOR ECONOMY AND PLANNING, MS. AZIZA BAROUD ANNOUNCED 
THAT AT LEAST 500,000 LOW INCOME PEOPLE WILL HAVEACCESS TO 
MICRO-CREDITS BETWEEN NOW AND 2013.  TE PLAN IS PART OF A GRAND 
POVERTY REDUCTION STRAEGY TO MAKE CREDIT AVAILABLE TO LOW INCOME 
CITIZNS WHO OTHERWISE DO NOT HAVE ACCESS TO REGULAR BAN LENDING.

FINANCE MINSTER FIRED 
------------------------ 
 
¶7.  FINANCE MINISTER DISMISSED. ON AUGUST 19 PRESIDENT DEBY 
UNEXPECTEDLY FIRED MINISTER OF FINANCE, MR. ABAKAR MALLAH MOURTCHA. 
ACCORDING TO L'OBSERVATEUR, A LOCAL NEWSPAPER, THE MINISTER WAS 
ACCUSED TO SELLING STATE LETTERS OF CREDIT TO A LOCAL BANK WITH THE 
AIM OF PERSONAL PROFIT, THUS CAUSING DELAY OF PAYMENTS TO THE 
RECIPIENTS OF THOSE LETTERS OF CREDIT, MOST OF WHOM ARE RETIRED 
CIVIL SERVANTS AND OTHER NEEDY PERSONS. SUCH CORRUPTION, ACCORDING 
TO THE NEWSPAPER, IS CONTRARY TO THE PRESIDENT'S DECLARED SOCIAL 
MANDATE.

INDIAN FIRMS INVEST IN CHAD 
---------------------------- 
 
¶8.  INDIAN COMPANIES TO INVEST IN THE CHADIAN AGRICULTURAL SECTOR.

WORLD BANK DELEGATION VISITS CHAD 
-------------------------------------- 
 
¶9.  A WORLD BANK DELEGATION LED BY ITS AFRICA REGION VICE PRESIDENT 
VISITED CHAD FROM AUGUST 23-25, 2008. ACCORDING TO A COMMUNIQUE 
DATED AUGUST 25 RELEASED BY THE MINISTRY OF ECONOMY AND PLANNING,

LITIGATION STEMING FROM THE WORLD BANK'S PARTICIPATION IN THE 
CHADIAN OIL PROJECT, AND TO CONSIDER RE-LAUNCHING THEIR COOPERATION 
AS SOON AS POSSIBLE, INCLUDING THE RE-OPENING OF THE BANK'S 
REPRESENTATION IN CHAD.  (THIS COULD JUST QUOTE THE COMMUNIQUE,

EUFOR AIR ASSETS 
------------------ 
 
¶10.  EUFOR UTILIZING HELICOPTERS TO EXTEND ITS REACH, WORKING WITH 
LOCAL TRADITIONAL AUTHORITIES TO COUNTER BANDITRY. THE PEACE-KEEPING 
EUROPEAN MISSION (EUFOR) ANNOUNCED IN A PRESS RELEASE THAT IT NOW 
HAS ELEVEN HELICOPTERS THAT WILL ENABLE ITS FORCES TO BETTER SECURE 
THE CONFLICT ZONE IN SPITE OF THE RAINY SEASON THAT HAS MADE ROADS 
IN THE REGION DIFFICULT TO NAVIGATE. ADDITIONALLY, THE EUFOR 
SPOKESMAN SAID THAT PATROLS HAVE BEEN WORKING WITH CHIEFS AND LOCAL 
AUTHORITIES IN ORDER TO REDUCE BANDITRY.  EUFOR CONTRIBUTING 
COUNTRIES ARE: FRANCE, IRELAND, POLAND, SWEDEN, AUSTRIA, ROMANIA, 
ITALY, BELGIUM, SPAIN, ALBANIA, FINLAND, PORTUGAL, SLOVENIA, THE 
NETHERLANDS, AND THE UNITED KINGDOM.
