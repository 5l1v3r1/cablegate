SUBJECT: Update on PKK Violence (February 8-15, 2009) 
 
REF: ANKARA 233 and previous 
 
(U) Sensitive but unclassified - please protect accordingly. 
 
¶1. (SBU) This is another in a series of weekly reports on PKK 
violence in Turkey.  Our primary source is the Turkish General Staff 
(TGS) website, which documents contacts and clashes with the PKK, as 
well as casualties incurred. 
 
¶2. (U) During the February 8-15, 2009, no security official was 
injured or killed.  The total number of security force casualties 
for the year through February 15, 2009, was zero killed and zero 
injured. 
 
¶3. (U) During the same period, no PKK terrorist was killed in 
clashes.  Three PKKers surrendered in Silopi, Sirnak Province and 
one was captured in Ozalp, Van Province on February 11.  According 
to official TGS website figures, through the second week of 
February, the number of PKK militants killed this year was zero, the 
number of those captured was five, and the number of those who 
surrendered was 14. 
 
¶4. (U) During the reporting period no civilians died in connection 
with PKK-linked violence, but four were wounded, including two in 
Guclukonak, Sirnak Province on February 9, one in Nusaybin, Mardin 
Province on February 14, and one in Uludere, Sirnak Province on 
February 15.  All four were injured when they stepped on PKK-planted 
landmines.  The total number of civilian casualties for the year 
through the end of the second week of February, was zero killed and 
five wounded.
