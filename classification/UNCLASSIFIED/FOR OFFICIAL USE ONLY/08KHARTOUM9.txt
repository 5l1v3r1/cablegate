SUBJECT: GONU EXTENDS CONDOLENCES ON KILLING OF U.S. DIPLOMAT 
 
¶1.  Post received a Diplomatic Note dated January 2, 2008 from the 
Presidency of the Republic of Sudan expressing condolences on the 
shooting death of USAID officer John Granville in Khartoum on New 
Year's morning.  Below is a non-official translation of the text of 
the Note, the original of which was in Arabic. 
 
¶2.  BEGIN TEXT OF DIP NOTE: 
 
January 2, 2008 
 
In the name of Presidential Advisor and Deputy Chief of the Eastern 
Front, Dr. Amna Mohamed Salih Dirar: 
 
We present our condolences to your Excellencies following the 
assassination of John Michael which happened yesterday and we regret 
this act which is contrary to human nature. 
 
We ask that you pass our sympathy on to the deceased's family, to 
your (colleagues at the) Embassy in Khartoum, and to all the people 
of the United States of America. 
 
Dr. Amna Mohamed Salih Dirar 
Presidential Advisor 
 
END TEXT 
 
¶3.  (SBU) COMMENT: The expression of condolences contained in the 
Diplomatic Note reflects the sentiments of the many Government 
officials and Sudanese citizens post has received through more 
informal channels.
