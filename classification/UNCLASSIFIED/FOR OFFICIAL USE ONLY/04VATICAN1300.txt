SUBJECT: HOLY SEE RECEPTIVE TO U.S. CONCERNS ON CHINA AND 
CUBA HUMAN RIGHTS 
 
REF: STATE 41252 
 
(SBU) POST SHARED REFTEL POINTS WITH ETTORE BALESTRERO, HOLY 
SEE'S DIRECTOR FOR HUMAN RIGHTS ISSUES APRIL 1.  BALESTRERO 
SAID THAT THE HOLY SEE DID NOT TYPICALLY TAKE AN OFFICIAL 
POSITION ON RESOLUTIONS CRITICIZING THE RECORDS OF PARTICULAR 
    COUNTRIES.  NEVERTHELESS, HE SAID, THE VATICAN REMAINED 
CONCERNED ABOUT THE HUMAN RIGHTS SITUATION IN BOTH CUBA AND 
CHINA -- PARTICULARLY REGARDING RELIGIOUS FREEDOM -- AND WAS 
SYMPATHETIC TO USG CONCERNS.  BALESTRERO PROMISED THAT THE 
HOLY SEE WOULD TAKE U.S. CONCERNS INTO ACCOUNT IN ITS 
DISCUSSIONS ON BOTH CUBA AND CHINA WITH INTERESTED COUNTRIES.
