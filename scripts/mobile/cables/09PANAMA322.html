SUBJECT: PANAMA: DEMARCHE ON KOSOVO'S IMF AND WORLD BANK 

MEMBERSHIP BID DELIVERED 

 

REF: STATE 33709

(C) "Having recognized Kosovo in January, Panama will have to act in a manner consistent with that decision and support Kosovo's membership in both the IMF and World Bank," Acting FM Javier Bonagas told POLCOUNS on April 17. Previously, Ambassador had sent First VP and FM Samuel Lewis and Minister of Economy and Finance letters on April 8 urging Panama to support fully Kosovo's membership in these IFIs. Due to top GOP officials' extensive travel over the past few weeks, post has had significant difficulty securing appointments for Ambassador to see Lewis and Alexander regarding this demarche. Ambassador did have the opportunity to flag this issue for Lewis briefly last week on the margins of a meeting concerning efforts to advance the U.S.-Panama Trade Promotion Agreement toward ratification, and POLCOUNS followed up to deliver reftel demarche. While not enthusiastic in expressing support for Kosovo's bid to become a member of the World Bank, Bonagas was resigned believing Panama, having recognized Kosovo, had no other alternative but to support Kosovar membership in the IFIs. Bonagas undertook to coordinate closely with the Ministry of Economy and Finance, which to date has not played a part in the MFA-led engagement on Kosovo. STEPHENSON
