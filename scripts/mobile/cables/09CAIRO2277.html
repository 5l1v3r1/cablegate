Blome for reason 1.4 (b).1.KEY POINTS -- (C) According to the MFA, the GOE's report for the UN Human Rights Council's (UNHRC) February 17 Universal Periodic Review (UPR) focuses on commitments on human trafficking legislation, a counterterrorism law to replace the State of Emergency, and limiting defamation cases. -- (C) The report also refers in general terms to NGO law amendments, the draft Uniform Places of Worship Law, and the definition of torture. -- (C) The report lists achievements such as press freedom, and notes obstacles such as the threat of terrorism and socio-economic problems. -- (C) The MFA wants to use the February 17 session to highlight its cooperation with UNHRC mechanisms, and is interested in a "respectful" exchange that would exclude discussion of sexual orientation discrimination.
